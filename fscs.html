First Come First Serve (FCFS) Scheduling

Introduction to FCFS Scheduling

First Come First Serve (FCFS) is the simplest CPU scheduling algorithm. The process that arrives first gets executed first, following the FIFO (First In, First Out) principle.

While easy to implement, it can lead to long waiting times for larger processes, causing the convoy effect.

Characteristics of FCFS

  • Non-Preemptive: Once a process starts execution, it runs till completion.
  • Fair Scheduling: The process that arrives first is served first.
  • Convoy Effect: Large processes may delay smaller ones, increasing average wait time.
  • Easy Implementation: Uses a simple queue structure.

How FCFS Works

FCFS operates by maintaining a queue of processes. When a process arrives, it is placed at the end of the queue and executed sequentially.

Example:

Consider three processes arriving at different times:

  • P1: Arrival: 0ms, Burst Time: 5ms
  • P2: Arrival: 1ms, Burst Time: 3ms
  • P3: Arrival: 2ms, Burst Time: 8ms

Execution order: P1 → P2 → P3
