Longest Job First (LJF) Scheduling

Introduction to LJF Scheduling

Longest Job First (LJF) is a scheduling algorithm that prioritizes processes with the longest burst time. This is essentially the opposite of SJF.

While not commonly used in general computing due to its inefficiency for reducing average waiting time, it can be useful in specific scenarios.

Characteristics of LJF

  • Non-Preemptive: Once a process starts execution, it runs till completion.
  • High Average Wait Time: Shorter processes wait longer behind longer ones.
  • Starvation: Short processes may be delayed indefinitely.
  • Maximizes Throughput: Can maximize throughput in specific batch processing scenarios.

How LJF Works

LJF selects the process with the longest burst time from the ready queue. If multiple processes arrive simultaneously, the one with the longest execution time gets priority.

Example:

Consider three processes arriving at different times:

  • P1: Arrival: 0ms, Burst Time: 5ms
  • P2: Arrival: 1ms, Burst Time: 3ms
  • P3: Arrival: 2ms, Burst Time: 8ms

Execution order: P1 → P3 → P2 (P1 starts first as it arrives at time 0, then P3 as it has longer burst time than P2)
